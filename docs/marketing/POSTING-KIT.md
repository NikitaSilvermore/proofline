# Milestamp — posting kit

The content engine for launching Milestamp. Two voices, one story. **Nothing
posts until Milestamp is live with real NLS students in it** — this kit is
loaded and ready for that moment, with real proof slotting into the marked
`[[slots]]`. Captions/carousels/reels live in [captions.md](captions.md).

---

## Who we're talking to

**High-ticket coaches, course creators, and the closers/setters who sell their
offers.** Nikita's `nikita.hts` follows them and they follow back — plus hundreds
of top closers on big offers. This audience feels three pains *daily*:

1. **Students pay, then ghost.** Momentum dies in week 2. Refund requests follow.
2. **No proof of results.** They *know* clients win, but can't show it — so the
   next sale is harder than it should be.
3. **Manual babysitting.** Chasing everyone in the group, guessing who's slipping.

Milestamp kills all three. That's the whole pitch.

## The one-liner

> **Milestamp turns a coaching programme into visible progress — students see
> their wins, you see who's slipping before they ghost, and results become proof
> you can actually sell with.**

Shorter: *the accountability + proof layer for high-ticket programmes.*

## The core message (say this a hundred ways)

**Results are the best marketing — but only if you can see them and show them.**
Milestamp makes progress visible to the student (so they stay) and to you (so you
save the ones slipping), then turns the wins into proof (so you sell the next
one). Retention and proof, on autopilot.

## Two voices

### @milestamp — the brand account (product-led, premium)
Confident, sleek, a little cinematic. The product *looks* expensive ("Midnight &
Brass" dark UI) — let it. Show the screens. Short lines. No hype words, no
exclamation-mark spam; the visuals carry the wow. Speaks to *offer owners*.

### nikita.hts — the founder account (build-in-public, peer-to-peer)
First person. You, a builder in their world, who got fed up and made the thing.
Opinionated, generous, specific. Tells the story and the lessons. Speaks *to
peers* — coaches and closers — as one of them. This account earns the trust;
@milestamp converts it.

**Golden rule:** never claim what isn't true yet. Pre-proof, the honest flex is
*"I built this / here's the thinking / here's the design."* Post-proof, you show
real student milestones (only from students who ticked the public-sharing consent
at intake — Milestamp captures that, so it's clean).

## Visuals — the product *is* the marketing

Milestamp is genuinely beautiful, so **screenshots and screen-records do the heavy
lifting.** Capture these (all on the dark theme):

- **Hero shot** — the progress page headline: *"From \$0 to \$[[X]] from the
  stage."* with the glowing gold graph. This one image sells it.
- **The milestone rail** — gold nodes, the pulsing "current" one.
- **The check-in celebration** — confetti over the dark card (great for a Reel).
- **The console** — the red/amber/green roster, glowing status pills ("30 seconds
  a day").
- **The intake stamp** — the gold "baseline locked" moment.

Screen-record flows for Reels: intake → gold stamp · a 45-sec check-in → confetti ·
opening the console and scanning the reds. Add captions; let the UI breathe.

**Brand note:** keep posts in the Milestamp world — deep ink, brass/gold, the
Bricolage/Spline type. Don't dilute it with bright stock graphics.
